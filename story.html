My Story

From simple lines to original abstract paintings.

Art started early, grew quickly, and became something serious. This page shares how that journey developed.

I started making art when I was ten years old. At first I drew lines on paper just for fun, but it did not take long before I started thinking more seriously about what I was creating.

By the time I was eleven, I had a plan. I wanted to sell my drawings, so I started offering them in the city center. That experience helped me grow creatively and made me want to keep going.

When I was twelve, I had my first art exhibition. Seeing my work on the wall was a big moment. Around that time, my art was also featured in a local magazine, which pushed me to make bigger and more detailed work.

Today my art has been shown in different places, including community spaces, a barber shop, and a social work organization building. Abstract art started with random lines, but now it is a big part of my life.
