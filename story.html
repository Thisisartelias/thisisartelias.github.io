WHO
THE F*CK
IS ELIAS?

Born in the Netherlands. Raised on loud music and ruined clothes.

There wasn't a profound "a-ha" moment where I realized I was an artist. I just couldn't sit still, and making a mess felt better than doing nothing.

I started painting because I couldn't afford therapy and I couldn't play guitar fast enough yet. The canvases became a dumping ground for whatever static was running through my head. At first it was purely destructive—throwing paint, ripping canvas. Eventually, I learned how to build structure out of the destruction.

The Audio Translation

Painting is quiet. Sometimes the studio is too quiet. I started writing music to fill the silence, and quickly realized that the music I was writing sounded exactly like the paintings I was making.

Alternative indie rock. Gritty, slightly unpolished, emotional. It's the exact same process as my abstract art: lay down a foundation, distort it, break it, and then try to make it beautiful again.

"I don't separate the art from the music. They are the same output, just using different frequencies."

Currently working out of a small, paint-splattered studio somewhere in the Netherlands. Preparing to launch my debut single and trying not to run out of cadmium red.
